#1 - V1V Creative Studio Website

What & Why

Build a cinematic, full-screen immersive website for V1V — a creative digital studio at v1v.in — matching and exceeding the visual quality of activetheory.net. The site uses real fully-dimensional 3D models (procedurally generated and/or imported GLTF/GLB assets), continuously animated in the Three.js scene, with WebGL post-processing, GSAP scroll-driven transitions, hand gesture support, and a password-protected admin panel. Nothing should look AI-generated, template-based, or generic — every element must feel handcrafted by a world-class creative studio.

3D Model Strategy

V1V logo disc + torus ring: Procedurally generated in Three.js — TorusGeometry with a physically-based glass material (MeshPhysicalMaterial: transmission=1, roughness=0.05, metalness=0.1, iridescence=1) — mirrors the AT ring exactly.
Wire/ribbon sculpture: Procedurally generated using TubeGeometry along a parametric lemniscate (figure-8) curve — thin tube with metallic/emissive material in cyan/blue/gold gradient.
Particle fields: Three.js Points with custom vertex shader — 8000+ particles with random sizes, soft additive blending, gentle float animation in useFrame loop.
Crystal formations on cards: Either procedurally generated using IcosahedronGeometry with vertex displacement shaders, or imported as free CC0 crystal/gem GLTF from Poly Pizza / KhronosGroup sample assets. These should be fully 3D and continuously rotating/floating.
Organic backbone/spine between cards: CatmullRomCurve3-based tube mesh with irregular radii — procedurally generated to look biological/organic.
Hexagonal tunnel: CylinderGeometry lined with instanced hexagonal prism meshes — procedurally built. Slow continuous rotation on Z-axis.
Industrial cage structure: Procedurally built from RingGeometry + CylinderGeometry pieces arranged as a cage — with glowing PointLight inside.
All models animate continuously in the useFrame render loop: slow rotation, gentle float (Math.sin wave), breathing scale pulses, and particle drift.
